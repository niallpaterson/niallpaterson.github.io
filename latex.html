LaTeX.

People often ask me if they can get a template of my handouts. I write in LaTeX, so if you want to use the handout style you'll need to install TeX and download a LaTeX editor. There are plenty of free editors available for Mac and Windows. If you are on a Mac and intend to use LaTeX consistently, I'd recommend Texpad. Its not as hard as it looks.

You might notice that my own handouts are a little 'fuller' than a standard handout. There's a reason for this: I follow the Mumford Method. The Mumford Method was developed by Stephen Mumford to foster inter-contintental academic research. Basically, its a kind of synoposis of a paper you are writing, the purpose of which is to get the structure of a paper down prior to drafting. I've found it useful when colloborating with others, but also for my own work. You write one document which doubles as both a draft and handout of the talk. I still put too much information in mine, but there's no need to. My handout template can be used as either a standard handout, or a Mumford sheet for paper drafting.

You can download the template below. I've also included my CV template. Feel free to use them for whatever purposes you want. You'll need almost no familiarity with the TeX language to use it, unless you want to edit the style to suit your own needs. You do need to learn hown to compile a document on your chosen editor. Knowledge of BibTex is required if you want to use citations. Basic instructions can be found in the comments at the top of the document.
